Deepfake: Detection Techniques & Digital Forensics

Authors

Mandadi Nikhit & Chintanippu Sai Pavani

MSc Cyber Security & Digital Forensics, Aditya University, Surampalem

Abstract

Deepfakes are AI-generated synthetic media created using models like GANs, autoencoders, and diffusion models. They pose threats such as misinformation, identity fraud, and social engineering. Modern detection requires multimodal analysis, AI-based classifiers, and biological signal detection.

Types of Deepfakes

  • Image Deepfakes
  • Video Deepfakes
  • Audio Deepfakes
  • Text-based Deepfakes

Deepfake in Digital Forensics

Deepfakes hinder forensic investigations by reducing metadata traces and pixel anomalies. Traditional methods struggle due to the realism of modern AI-generated media.

Detection Techniques

  • Visual Artifact Detection
  • Video-Based Temporal Analysis
  • Audio-Visual Synchronization
  • Frequency Domain Analysis
  • Deep Learning Detection Models
  • Metadata & Digital Forensics

Challenges in Detection

  • Insufficient datasets
  • Adversarial attacks
  • Temporal aggregation issues
  • Limited labeled data

Conclusion & Future Trends

Deepfakes challenge digital trust and cybersecurity. Future detection approaches include explainable AI, blockchain authentication, and real-time forensic systems.
